About Me

Designing where
complexity lives

I came into UX through front-end code — which means I've always designed with real constraints in mind. Eight years later, I work in the spaces most designers avoid: complex government systems, internal banking tools, enterprise logistics — where "good UX" is genuinely hard.

I run discovery, I build systems, I fight for the user in rooms where business requirements pull in the other direction — and I deliver work that actually ships.

Outside of work, I'm a mum living in Frisco, TX — which gives you a very sharp sense of what "intuitive" actually means.

Logistics E-Commerce Usability Testing Nov 2018 – Aug 2021
FedEx Office Category UX

Product category pages had high drop-off — customers landing on the right product were still leaving. I developed two competing PDP concepts with different approaches to pricing, then ran moderated usability sessions with 10 participants. Concept B won for reasons we didn't expect. My developer instincts became a UX advantage here.

I didn't start in UX. I started writing code — and somewhere between building interfaces and watching people struggle to use them, I realised the problem wasn't the code. It was the design.

That pivot, about 8 years ago, is still the best decision I've made professionally. I work at the intersection I've always cared about: where complex systems meet real people who just need things to work.

Outside of work, I'm a mum in Frisco, TX — which gives you a very sharp sense of what "intuitive" actually means.
