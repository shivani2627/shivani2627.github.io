How I Design

Design Principles

Six principles that guide every project, regardless of industry or platform.

🔍
Research Before Pixels

Every design decision starts with understanding real users in their real context. I don’t design from assumptions — I design from evidence gathered through interviews, shadowing, and testing.

⚡
Clarity Over Cleverness

The best interface is the one users don’t have to think about. I favour clear hierarchy, obvious affordances, and predictable patterns over visual novelty that creates friction.

⚖
Accessibility by Default

Accessible design is good design for everyone. WCAG 2.1 AA is a baseline, not a checkbox — colour contrast, focus states, and screen-reader compatibility are built in from the start.

🆕
Systems Thinking

I design components, not just screens. Every element belongs to a larger system — reusable, documented, and scalable so teams can build consistently without reinventing the wheel.

👥
Collaborate to Ship

Design doesn’t end at handoff. I embed with engineering through build, run QA against specs, and stay involved until the experience in production matches the intent in the design file.

📊
Measure What Matters

Good UX is measurable. Task time, error rates, NPS, and usability test scores all tell a story. I track outcomes so design decisions can be validated, iterated, and communicated to stakeholders.
