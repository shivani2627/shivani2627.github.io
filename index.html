My Process

What process you can expect from me

Not a template. A discipline built across 8 years of embedded work — government, enterprise, banking. Every engagement follows the same rigour, regardless of timeline or scope.

🤝
01
Stakeholder meetings & notes

Every engagement starts with a structured session — not a kickoff call, an actual working meeting. I prepare a discussion guide in advance, document decisions and open questions, and share a written summary within 24 hours. Nothing falls through the cracks.

Discussion guideSession notesOpen questions logStakeholder map
🔍
02
UX/UI audit of the feature

Before designing anything new, I audit what exists. Heuristic evaluation across all flows, cataloguing inconsistencies, friction points, accessibility failures, and design debt. This is what prevents redesigns that recreate the same problems in a new visual style.

Heuristic evaluationFriction inventoryAccessibility auditDesign debt log
📊
03
Review analytics

Numbers tell you where users stop — they don't tell you why. I review drop-off rates, funnel data, error logs, and support tickets alongside qualitative research so the two inform each other. Analytics without context is noise.

Funnel analysisDrop-off mappingError log reviewSupport ticket themes
📝
04
Note findings, update PRD

Findings from research, audit, and analytics get synthesised into a single source of truth. I update the PRD with user insights alongside product requirements — so the team builds from evidence, not assumptions, and traceability is preserved.

Insights synthesisPRD annotationsEvidence-linked requirementsDecision log
👥
05
Conduct research

Contextual inquiry, moderated usability testing, and stakeholder interviews with real users — not proxies. Sessions documented with participant quotes. Synthesis in FigJam: affinity maps, journey maps, persona frameworks. Output is a prioritised insight doc, not a 60-slide deck.

Contextual inquiryUsability testingAffinity mappingJourney mapInsight summary
📰
06
Read forums, blogs & trends

I research how similar problems have been solved in comparable domains — competitor products, adjacent industries, emerging patterns. Government UX borrows from healthcare UX. Enterprise dashboards borrow from fintech. Context-reading keeps solutions from being isolated.

Competitor auditIndustry benchmarksPattern librariesDomain research
💡
07
Conduct research & ideate

Synthesis becomes generation. I run ideation sessions with cross-functional teams — structured exercises, not free-for-alls. How Might We framing, opportunity mapping, concept sketches. Every idea is grounded in a specific user need surfaced from research.

HMW framingOpportunity mappingConcept explorationCross-team ideation
🧠
08
Brainstorm ideas

Before screens, possibilities. I explore multiple directions at low fidelity — diverging deliberately before converging. This is where unconventional approaches get considered. The best solution in my portfolio case studies rarely came from the first obvious direction.

Divergent explorationDirection comparisonFeasibility checkConcept selection
✏️
09
Sketching

Paper first. Always. Sketching is thinking — faster feedback loops, no visual polish to get attached to, and stakeholders give more honest reactions to rough sketches than polished wireframes. I sketch key screens and flows before touching Figma.

Paper sketchesFlow diagramsLayout explorationStakeholder sketch review
🖥️
10
Digitised into UI

Sketches become wireframes, wireframes become high-fidelity — built on a component library from day one. Every component is named, documented, and variant-ed. Colour tokens, type scales, spacing grids. I design the system, not just the screens. Annotated handoff specs cover every state, edge case, and responsive behaviour. My front-end background means I can have a real conversation about what's feasible — and what's worth fighting for.

Component libraryDesign tokensHi-fi screensAnnotated specsWCAG AA auditDev handoff
