Government · Public Sector · Jan 2026–Present · State of Missouri
Missouri DESE School Finance
The problem Finance staff at 500+ districts were submitting school data through a 2003-era system — 4+ hours per submission, zero error guidance, and no way to save progress. My job was to fix that without losing the existing business logic.
Led end-to-end UX from discovery to React handoff. Ran interviews with 12 finance directors across rural and urban districts to understand where breakdowns really happened — then redesigned the submission flow around their actual mental model, not the legacy database structure. Built a 40+ component design system so the engineering team could move fast without breaking accessibility.

Case Management · District Attorneys · Jan 2024–Jun 2025
PROTECT Case Management
The problem District Attorneys across Wisconsin were managing active cases in spreadsheets and paper files. A new platform was needed — but DAs are not typical software users. Getting the UX right meant understanding a workflow most designers have never touched.
Embedded with the Wisconsin DOJ team for 1.5 years. Designed the brand identity, information architecture, and all 50+ screens across web and desktop — including a complex eFiling portal and XCQ query interface. I ran design reviews with DAs, adjusted when desktop constraints conflicted with web assumptions, and built a pattern library so future screens stayed consistent.
🔍 Stakeholder workshops 🖥️ Desktop-first UX 📁 eFiling flows 🎨 Brand identity

Banking · Internal Dashboard · Nov 2021–Jul 2022 · Synechron
Citibank Spotlight
The problem Six different Citi teams were tracking project status across four separate internal tools. No single view, no clear ownership, constant "who owns this?" meetings. The ask: one portal, every module, instant clarity.
Designed the Spotlight Dashboard end-to-end within Citi's existing design system — which meant working within strict brand constraints while still solving a genuine UX problem. I mapped the IA around module ownership as the primary mental model (not project type, which was the original brief). Delivered full dark and light modes, both WCAG AA compliant.

Enterprise · Shipping · Oct 2022–Apr 2023 · Wipro
HP MPS Shipping Dashboard
The problem HP's managed print services team had no real-time visibility into shipment status across hundreds of enterprise devices. Sales and contract managers were working blind — decisions were made on stale data from disconnected systems.
Designed two connected enterprise platforms — PMPS for the sales pipeline and CMPS for contract management — both feeding live shipment data. The challenge was presenting complex logistics data without overwhelming users who check dashboards while doing five other things. Heavy use of Axure prototypes to validate data hierarchy decisions with actual HP managers before development.

Logistics · E-Commerce · Nov 2018–Aug 2021 · Atos
FedEx Office Category UX
The problem FedEx Office's product category pages had high drop-off — customers landing on the right product were still leaving without purchasing. The heuristic audit pointed to pricing confusion and weak product hierarchy, but we needed real user evidence before redesigning.
My first professional UX project — and the one that showed me how much research changes the design. I developed two competing PDP concepts with different approaches to pricing presentation, then ran moderated usability sessions with 10 participants across the US. Concept B won, but not for the reasons we expected — the insight came from watching, not assuming. This is also where my developer instincts first became a UX advantage.
🔬 Heuristic audit 👁️ Moderated testing 🆚 A/B concepts 📱 Responsive design

I didn't start in UX. I started writing code — and somewhere between building interfaces and watching people struggle to use them, I realised the problem wasn't the code. It was the design.

That pivot, about 8 years ago, is still the best decision I've made professionally. I now get to work at the intersection I've always cared about: where complex systems meet real people who just need things to work.

Outside of work, I'm a mum living in Frisco, TX — which, if you've ever tried to design a simple experience while also managing a toddler's chaos, gives you a genuinely sharp sense for what "intuitive" actually means.
