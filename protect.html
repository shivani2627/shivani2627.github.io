Project Overview

Wisconsin DAs were running active cases in spreadsheets

The Wisconsin Department of Justice needed a purpose-built case management system for their District Attorneys — the PROTECT Case Management platform. Before PROTECT, DAs and investigators tracked active criminal cases in spreadsheets, shared documents, and paper files. Data was siloed, handoffs were error-prone, and there was no single source of truth for case status.

I was brought in as the sole UI/UX designer for 1.5 years, responsible for all five major design workstreams: brand identity, desktop app revamp, details pane web app, XCQ web querying, and eFiling portal.

Discovery Process

The real problem wasn't the missing tool

Before designing a single screen, I ran a series of stakeholder workshops with District Attorneys, investigators, legal assistants, and the IT team to map the full case lifecycle — from initial filing through court dates, evidence tracking, and closure.

The workshops revealed a critical insight that changed the entire design direction:

Key Insight
"Desktop users (investigators) and web users (DAs in court) had fundamentally different interaction patterns — desktop users needed dense, scannable grids; web users needed quick status views on small screens. Building one system to serve both would have failed both."

This insight drove the entire information architecture decision — two distinct interaction models within one connected data system, rather than one compromise interface.

Five Workstreams

One platform, five major efforts

Over 1.5 years, I led design across five distinct workstreams under the PROTECT umbrella:

01
Brand Identity
Designed the PROTECT logo, color system, and visual language from scratch. Required balancing official government aesthetics with approachable usability for busy legal staff.
02
Desktop App Revamp
Rebuilt the existing desktop case management interface. Focused on dense data tables, keyboard navigation, and fast workflows for investigators who live in this tool all day.
03
Details Pane Web App
Designed the web-based case details view for DAs accessing PROTECT from court or remote locations. Simplified to high-signal status information on any screen size.
04
XCQ Web Querying
Designed a query interface for cross-case data retrieval. Applied consistent patterns from the desktop app to ensure investigators could switch contexts without relearning.
05
eFiling Portal
End-to-end design for the electronic filing portal, including form architecture, error states, confirmation flows, and integration with existing court filing systems.
Design Approach

Embedded, not adjacent — design inside the team

Being embedded for 1.5 years meant I wasn't just a designer handing off specs. I attended sprint planning, joined standups, reviewed builds in staging, and pushed back when implementation diverged from intent. My front-end background meant conversations with engineering were direct and precise.

With a government client, accessibility wasn't optional. Every screen was designed and reviewed for WCAG 2.1 AA compliance, keyboard navigation, and screen reader support — requirements that shaped design decisions from color choice through interaction patterns.

PROTECT went from a concept to a shipped, working case management platform — replacing the spreadsheet-and-paper system that DAs and investigators had relied on for years. The dual interaction model (desktop-dense vs. web-simplified) was validated by the legal team and adopted without major revisions.
