Overview

The Challenge

The Missouri Department of Elementary and Secondary Education manages the financial records of over 500 school districts, handling everything from annual budget submissions to monthly payroll reporting and fund accounting. The legacy system — over a decade old — had accumulated layers of complexity that made routine tasks slow, error-prone, and deeply frustrating for the administrators who depended on it daily.

I was brought in to lead a full end-to-end UX redesign: from initial research and discovery through a production-ready design system and component library that the engineering team could build directly from.

⚠️
Before — Key Pain Points
Inconsistent UI across modules with no shared component language. District administrators averaged 18 minutes per monthly report submission. Error rates on fund transfers exceeded 12%. No mobile usability. System required 3+ browser tabs open simultaneously for common workflows.
✓
After — Measurable Outcomes
Unified design system with 40+ reusable components. Monthly report submission reduced to under 8 minutes. Data entry error rate dropped below 5%. Full WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility compliance. Single-page workflows with persistent context via sidebar navigation.
Process

How I Approached It

01 — Discover
Research & Audit
12 stakeholder interviews, heuristic audit of 8 core workflows, shadowing sessions with district finance administrators across 3 different district sizes.
02 — Define
Synthesis & IA
Affinity mapping revealed 4 core persona types. Rebuilt information architecture around task frequency, not org structure. Defined role-based access model (DESE, District, Public).
03 — Design
System & Prototyping
Established brand tokens, typography scale, and 40+ component library in Figma. Built interactive Axure prototypes for usability testing. Iterated over 3 rounds of testing.
04 — Deliver
Handoff & Build
Delivered annotated Figma specs + React/TypeScript/Tailwind component library. Embedded with engineering team through build phase, conducting weekly QA reviews.
Research

What Users Actually Said

Stakeholder interviews surfaced consistent themes around cognitive overload, lack of visual hierarchy, and mistrust in the system's accuracy due to unclear feedback states. Three key insights shaped the design direction.

"I keep three windows open at the same time because I can never remember which year I'm looking at. It's easy to submit the wrong data without realizing it."
District Finance Director · Large District
"I don't know when something is saved vs. just typed. Half the time I re-enter everything from scratch because I'm not sure it went through."
Business Manager · Small District
"Every module looks completely different. I never know where the submit button will be or what the table columns mean."
DESE Program Coordinator
