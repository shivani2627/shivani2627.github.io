Overview

Two Platforms, One Ecosystem

HP’s Managed Print Services business manages print fleets for enterprise clients — handling everything from hardware contracts and consumable supply chains to shipment logistics and on-site service. The challenge was that the sales and contract lifecycle existed across disconnected tools, creating friction for both HP sales teams and client account managers.

I designed two interconnected platforms that cover the complete MPS journey: PMPS (Prospective MPS) — a sales and opportunity management tool for bringing new accounts onto HP contracts — and CMPS (Contract MPS) — an operational dashboard for managing active contracts, fleet shipments, supply orders, and delivery performance once a client is on contract.

⚠ Before — Key Pain Points
  • Sales teams managed opportunities in spreadsheets with no shared visibility
  • Proposal creation was manual, inconsistent, and disconnected from customer data
  • Active contract managers had no real-time view of fleet shipment status
  • Supply reorders required multiple system logins and manual tracking
  • Fleet data was siloed — no single dashboard for device health + shipping + spend
  • No clear handoff between the sales (PMPS) and operational (CMPS) phases
✓ After — Design Outcomes
  • Unified PMPS platform covers Opportunities → Proposals → Customer onboarding
  • CMPS gives contract managers live shipment tracking, fleet data, and spend analytics
  • Single MPC Dashboard as the home for all active contract activity
  • Consistent IA and component language shared across both platforms
  • Shipping status, delivery performance, and supply workflows fully integrated
  • Axure RP prototypes used for stakeholder validation before development

Platform Overview

PMPS & CMPS — What I Designed

The two platforms are distinct but connected. PMPS handles the pre-contract sales journey; CMPS handles the operational reality of managing an active print fleet contract — including shipment logistics, fleet health, and supply management.

📈 PMPS — Prospective MPS
The sales-side platform. Used by HP account managers to manage the full opportunity-to-contract pipeline for prospective enterprise print customers.
  • Opportunity creation and pipeline management
  • Proposal builder with product and service configuration
  • Customer profile creation and data management
  • Customer Opportunity Management linking accounts to deals
  • Onboarding workflows to transition won accounts to CMPS
Open PMPS Prototype
🚚 CMPS — Contract MPS
The operational platform. Used by contract managers and logistics coordinators to manage active HP print fleet accounts — tracking shipments, device health, and supply orders.
  • MPC Dashboard — live view of all active contracts and fleet status
  • Shipment tracking with route visibility and delivery performance
  • Fleet Data Management — device inventory and health across sites
  • Supply reorder workflows triggered by usage thresholds
  • Monthly shipping spend analytics and SLA monitoring

Key Screens

Designed Screens

The interactive Axure prototypes are the primary artefact for both platforms. Each screen below describes the design decisions and UX rationale — open the live prototype to explore the full interaction.

01
MPC Dashboard — The Command Centre
Open in CMPS
The home page surfaces everything a contract manager needs at a glance: active shipments count, pending dispatch alerts, delivery performance rate, and monthly spend — all without navigating away. The dashboard acts as a coordination hub, surfacing urgent actions (exceptions, low-stock alerts) alongside steady-state metrics. Navigation to all six modules flows from this single entry point, creating a consistent mental model for users across both PMPS and CMPS workflows.
KPI cards — live data Exception alerts surfaced Single entry point for all 6 modules CMPS Platform
02
Opportunities — Sales Pipeline Management
Open in PMPS
The Existing Opportunities view gives HP account managers a filterable, sortable list of all active and historical opportunities. Each row shows the account name, stage, assigned rep, proposal status, and expected close date — giving sales managers the visibility they previously managed in spreadsheets. The Create Opportunity flow walks reps through account selection, service configuration, and estimated fleet size to generate a qualified lead record.
Replaces spreadsheet tracking Pipeline stage visibility Filter by rep, stage, date PMPS Platform
03
Proposals — Structured Proposal Builder
Open in PMPS
The proposal builder standardises what was previously an ad-hoc, inconsistent process. Reps configure device types, service levels, contract length, and supply terms within a guided multi-step flow. Existing Proposals provides a library of past proposals that can be duplicated and modified for similar accounts — significantly reducing creation time. Every proposal links back to its parent opportunity and customer record.
Guided multi-step flow Duplicate & modify existing Linked to opportunity + customer PMPS Platform
04
Fleet Data Management — The Logistics Core
Open in CMPS
Fleet Data Management is the operational centrepiece of CMPS and the most logistics-intensive screen in the system. Contract managers see live device inventory across all client sites, real-time shipment status for toner and consumable orders, delivery performance metrics (on-time rate, exceptions), and a monthly shipping spend breakdown. Shipment rows show tracking numbers, contents, destination, ETA, and status — replacing the multi-system login that previously required contract managers to context-switch between HP tools and carrier portals.
Live shipment tracking Delivery performance metrics Device inventory across sites Monthly spend analytics CMPS Platform
05
Onboarding — PMPS to CMPS Handoff
Full Project
The Onboarding module is the bridge between the two platforms — where a won opportunity transitions from the PMPS sales pipeline into an active CMPS contract. My Customers gives each rep a personal view of their accounts in onboarding; All Customers gives managers a team-wide view. This handoff was a critical UX design challenge: the data, terminology, and user mental model shifts from “prospect” to “client” at this point, and the design needed to reflect that transition without creating confusion.
