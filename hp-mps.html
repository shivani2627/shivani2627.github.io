Project Overview

Enterprise UX for
HP's Print Platform

At HP, I was responsible for the full UI/UX design of the Managed Print Services platform — a complex enterprise tool used to manage print environments across large organizations. The project required mastering Axure RP 10 from scratch to meet the team's tooling requirements.

🎯
The Challenge
Design a comprehensive enterprise print management interface that lets administrators monitor, configure, and manage print devices across large distributed organizations — while learning a new prototyping tool mid-project.
⚡
The Approach
Self-taught Axure RP 10 to meet the project's specific tooling requirements. Delivered 30+ screens covering the full product scope — from device monitoring dashboards to configuration flows and reporting interfaces.
📐
Scope
30+ screens covering dashboards, device management, user administration, reporting, and configuration workflows. Complete end-to-end UX — from initial wireframes through high-fidelity interactive prototypes.
✅
Outcome
Delivered the complete UX design on schedule, demonstrating the ability to adapt quickly to new tools and complex enterprise design constraints without compromising quality or timeline.
Design Process

How I
Approached It

01
Tool Mastery
Self-taught Axure RP 10 from scratch to meet the project's specific tooling requirements — learning advanced prototyping, dynamic panels, conditional logic, and interaction design within the platform.
02
Requirements & Research
Gathered requirements from HP stakeholders, reviewed enterprise print management conventions, and mapped the core user workflows that the platform needed to support at scale.
03
Wireframing & Architecture
Developed the information architecture and low-fidelity wireframes for all 30+ screens, establishing navigation patterns, data hierarchy, and interaction flows before moving to high fidelity.
04
High-Fidelity Prototypes
Built fully interactive high-fidelity prototypes in Axure RP 10 — delivered to the HP team for review, feedback, and developer handoff within the project timeline.
