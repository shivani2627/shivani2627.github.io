Overview

Two Platforms, One Ecosystem

HP’s Managed Print Services business manages enterprise print fleets for large corporate clients — handling hardware contracts, consumable supply chains, shipment logistics, and on-site service. The problem was that the complete sales-to-contract lifecycle was fragmented across disconnected tools: spreadsheets for opportunity tracking, email threads for proposals, and multiple portals for shipment status.

I designed two platforms from end to end: PMPS covers the pre-contract sales journey — opportunity management, proposal building, and customer onboarding. CMPS covers the operational reality once a client is on contract — live fleet management, shipment tracking, supply reorders, and delivery performance analytics. Together they form the MPC (Managed Print Contracts) ecosystem.

⚠ Before — Key Pain Points
  • Sales teams managed opportunities in spreadsheets with no shared pipeline visibility
  • Proposals created ad-hoc — inconsistent format, disconnected from customer data
  • Active contract managers had no real-time view of fleet shipment status
  • Supply reorders required multiple system logins and manual tracking across portals
  • Fleet data siloed — no single dashboard for device health, shipping, and spend
  • No defined handoff between the sales (PMPS) and operational (CMPS) phases
✓ After — Design Outcomes
  • PMPS unifies Opportunities → Proposals → Customer creation in one platform
  • CMPS gives contract managers live shipment tracking, fleet data, and spend analytics
  • Single MPC Dashboard home for all active contract and shipping activity
  • Consistent IA and shared component language across both platforms
  • Onboarding module creates a defined, visible PMPS → CMPS handoff path
  • Axure RP 10 prototypes used for stakeholder sign-off before development

Design Process

How I Approached It

With two interconnected enterprise platforms to design, I needed a disciplined process. I worked sequentially — locking the IA and shared component foundations before designing module by module, validating with stakeholders using Axure prototypes at each stage.

01
Discovery
Stakeholder Research & Requirements
Ran stakeholder interviews with HP sales reps, contract managers, and their managers to map current workflows, pain points, and must-have features. Documented the existing tools being replaced and the data flows between them. Created a requirements matrix mapping each user need to a platform feature.
Stakeholder interviews Workflow mapping Requirements matrix Pain point audit
02
Define
Information Architecture & Platform Split
The key structural decision was splitting the product into two distinct platforms with a shared entry point — rather than one monolithic tool. I mapped the full MPS contract lifecycle and defined which stages belonged to PMPS (pre-contract) and which to CMPS (active contract). The MPC Dashboard site map was designed in Draw.io and validated with stakeholders before any wireframing began.
MPC site map User journey maps Platform split decision Navigation structure
03
Design
Component System & Module Design
Before designing individual screens I established the shared component language — the same nav chrome, table style, badge system, form inputs, and filter bar used across both platforms. Then I designed module by module: Opportunities and Proposals in PMPS first, followed by Fleet Data Management and Dashboard in CMPS. This order ensured the most complex screens (fleet + shipment tracking) were built on a proven foundation. All designs produced as interactive Axure RP 10 prototypes.
Component system Axure RP prototypes Module wireframes Interaction flows
04
Validate
Stakeholder Reviews & Iteration
Each module was reviewed with HP stakeholders using the clickable Axure prototypes. Feedback was collected, prioritised, and iterated in-file before moving to the next module. The Onboarding module required the most iteration — the handoff between PMPS and CMPS was a new concept for users and required two rounds of revision to get the mental model right.

Supported the development team through build, answering design questions and reviewing implementations against the prototype. The Axure Cloud share link was used as the living spec.

Key Screens

Real Wireframes

These are the actual Axure RP wireframes from the project — the artefacts used for stakeholder reviews, development briefings, and sign-off. Each screen reflects a specific design decision explained below.

01
Dashboard — Managed Print Central Home
Open Prototype
The Dashboard is the command centre for the entire MPS platform. The design surfaces six distinct data widgets simultaneously — Customer Lookup, Active Proposals bar chart, Recent Proposals, Recent Opportunities, Alternate Proposals, In Progress Proposals, and Approved Proposals — giving account managers a full picture of their pipeline without navigating away. The Active Proposals chart (In Progress / Approved / Alternative / Declined) is the most prominent element, giving instant visual feedback on proposal health. The “Customize” control allows users to personalise their widget layout — a direct response to the research finding that different roles needed different information prioritised. The top navigation bar (Account, Customer, History, Go To, Regions) reflects the enterprise context: users operate across multiple customers and regions simultaneously.
6 dashboard widgets — full pipeline at a glance Active Proposals bar chart — proposal health signal Customisable widget layout per user role Customer Lookup — direct account access PMPS & CMPS shared home
02
Opportunities — Archived Opportunities View
PMPS Prototype
The Opportunities module uses a three-tab structure: Create Opportunity, Existing Opportunities, and Archived Opportunities. This screen shows the Archived view — showing historical opportunities with statuses of Active, Lost, and Abandoned. Several key UX decisions are visible here: the “Won / All” toggle filter at the top lets users instantly switch between won-only and all archived records — a high-frequency filter that warranted a toggle rather than a dropdown. Page size control and global search are positioned consistently across all list views. The data table columns (Opportunity ID, Description, Status, Customer Name, Validity Expiration Date, Number of Proposals, Migration Date) are horizontally scrollable — the right-side scroll indicator signals more columns are available without cluttering the visible area. Pagination (“Page 1 of 20”) keeps the interface performant at scale.
Won/All toggle — high-frequency filter as toggle not dropdown Horizontal scroll for dense column data 3-tab structure: Create / Existing / Archived Pagination for enterprise-scale data PMPS Platform
03
Proposals — Create Proposal Form
PMPS Prototype
The Create Proposal form is intentionally minimal — six fields that capture everything needed to create a proposal record: Customer, Opportunity (linked), Program Type, Contract Type, Proposal Description, and a Coterminous checkbox. The design decision to keep this form short was deliberate: the research showed that a bloated creation form was the primary reason reps abandoned proposal creation mid-way and reverted to email. By stripping it to the essential identifiers only, and linking Customer and Opportunity via inline edit controls (pencil icon), the form becomes a fast intake screen — additional detail is added in subsequent steps after the record exists. The blue “Create Proposal” CTA is the only action on the page, making the intent unambiguous. The three-tab structure (Create Proposal active, Existing Proposals, Archived Proposals) maintains context without requiring navigation away.
Minimal 6-field form — intentional to reduce abandonment Inline edit controls link Customer + Opportunity Single CTA — no ambiguity about next action Coterminous checkbox — enterprise-specific requirement PMPS Platform
04
Proposals — Existing Proposals with Status Filters
PMPS Prototype
The Existing Proposals view is the most filter-rich screen in PMPS. Two filter tiers sit above the table: an “Active / All” opportunity filter toggle (consistent with the Opportunities module), and a multi-select Proposal Status row with checkboxes for Approved, Alternate, In Progress, Rejected, Won, Archived, and All. This two-tier approach was a deliberate design choice — the toggle addresses the most common filter (active vs. all), while the checkboxes enable power users to construct precise multi-status views without the overhead of a full filter panel. The data table exposes 10 columns including Proposal Number, Description, Status, Customer Name, Date Created, Program, Proposal Content, Contract Type, Price Validity End Date, and Opportunity Status — all relevant to the enterprise review workflow. Horizontal scrolling handles column density without sacrificing data. The green-starred bookmark icons on Proposals and Opportunities in the nav are a consistent favourite/shortcut affordance visible across the platform.
Two-tier filtering: toggle + multi-select checkboxes 7 proposal status filters — Approved, Alternate, In Progress, Rejected, Won, Archived, All 10 data columns — horizontal scroll for density Bookmark star — favourites shortcut in nav PMPS Platform
